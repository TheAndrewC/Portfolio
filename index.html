Hello, my name is Andrew Caballero

How I'm Staying in Touch

In the midst of the Covid-19 global health pandemic, we were all essencially forced to move back home and are isolated from friends and extended family. While this situation also can be lonely, I have found many ways to keep in touch with my family, friends and school groupd.

Obviously, all of our classes are over Zoom; however, besides using them for classes, I have also used it to most game nights for my friends. Apps like Facetime are great, but are Apple-device exclusive. By using Zoom and free, third-party games like scribble.io or spyfall, I have been able to host large get-togethers with my friends and family. Additionally, there are extensions that can let you watch your favorite Netflix shows and movies synchronously with your friends, and Hulu has just built in a similar, native feature.

In addition to only communicating with friends, many of the clubs that I was involved with at school are creating virtual meetings using Facebook and Zoom. They post meeting times, information, and Zoom links on Facebook, then we can still hold our meetings over Zoom.

During this pandemic, we have also seen mass protests towards police violence against people of color. During this time, it is more important than ever to communicate with others, and communicate the need for reform. No matter whether it is through Zoom, ralleys, Facebook, or any other platform, it is imparative to stay connected to each other and to the world around us.
